  • 1: Novice
    • Indicates a basic understanding of the subject matter.
    • Possesses minimal knowledge or experience in the field.
    • May struggle with fundamental concepts and applications.
  • 2: Beginner
    • Demonstrates some familiarity with the subject.
    • Understands basic principles but lacks depth in knowledge.
    • Requires guidance and further learning to grasp more advanced topics.
  • 3: Intermediate
    • Shows a solid understanding of core concepts and principles.
    • Can apply knowledge to solve moderately complex problems.
    • Has a good grasp of foundational theories but may lack expertise in specialized areas.
  • 4: Advanced
    • Exhibits expertise in the subject matter.
    • Possesses comprehensive knowledge and skills.
    • Capable of tackling complex issues and contributing original insights.
    • Often engaged in ongoing learning and stays updated with developments in the field.
  • 5: Expert
    • Represents the highest level of proficiency and mastery.
    • Demonstrates exceptional knowledge, skills, and experience.
    • Considered a leader or authority in the subject area.
    • Contributes significantly to research, innovation, or practice within the field.
    • Often sought after for consultation, teaching, or leadership roles.
